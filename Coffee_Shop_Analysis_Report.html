01 — Overview
What This Project Is About

This project performs a comprehensive sales analysis for a coffee shop chain operating across multiple Indian cities. Using MySQL, it answers 10 business-critical questions to help leadership make data-driven decisions about where to open new outlets, which products to stock, and how efficiently each city operates.

The analysis covers market sizing, revenue performance, product popularity, customer behavior, cost vs. earnings comparison, and sales trends — ultimately producing a final city recommendation for expansion.

04 — SQL Highlights
Key Queries Explained

Q6 — Top 3 Products Per City (Window Function)

Uses a CTE + DENSE_RANK() partitioned by city to find top-performing products.
WITH CTE AS (
  SELECT city_name, product_name,
         COUNT(sale_id) AS total_orders,
         DENSE_RANK() OVER(
           PARTITION BY city_name
           ORDER BY COUNT(sale_id) DESC
         ) AS RNK
  FROM products
  INNER JOIN sales ON sales.product_id = products.product_id
  INNER JOIN customers ON customers.customer_id = sales.customer_id
  INNER JOIN city ON city.city_id = customers.city_id
  GROUP BY city_name, product_name
)
SELECT * FROM CTE WHERE RNK <= 3;

Q9 — Monthly Sales Growth Rate (LAG Function)

Two-CTE approach: first compute monthly revenue, then apply LAG() to get previous month for % change.
WITH monthly_sales AS (
  SELECT city_name,
         YEAR(sale_date) AS year_sale,
         MONTH(sale_date) AS month_sale,
         SUM(total) AS monthly_revenue
  FROM sales
  INNER JOIN customers ON sales.customer_id = customers.customer_id
  INNER JOIN city ON city.city_id = customers.city_id
  GROUP BY city_name, YEAR(sale_date), MONTH(sale_date)
),
previous_month AS (
  SELECT *,
         LAG(monthly_revenue) OVER
           (PARTITION BY city_name ORDER BY year_sale, month_sale)
           AS previous_month_sales
  FROM monthly_sales
)
SELECT city_name, year_sale, month_sale,
       ROUND(((monthly_revenue - previous_month_sales)
         / previous_month_sales) * 100, 2) AS sales_growth_rate
FROM previous_month;
05 — Insights
Key Business Takeaways
📊

Market Sizing Matters

Cities with large populations but low unique customer counts represent untapped markets — prioritize marketing there before opening new outlets.

💸

Revenue ≠ Profitability

High-revenue cities like Bangalore are offset by very high rent. Avg rent per customer is the real profitability indicator, not total revenue alone.

📈

Growth Trend is Key

A city with moderate revenue but consistent month-over-month growth is a better expansion bet than a city with high revenue but declining sales.

☕

Product Mix by City

Top 3 products vary by city. Stocking city-specific bestsellers instead of a uniform menu can significantly boost sales and reduce wastage.
